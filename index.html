Why should you vote?

Voting is an important civic duty we have as American citizens. The Fifteenth Amendment to the US Constitutional says that "[t]he right of citizens of the United States to vote shall not be denied or abridged by the United States or by any State on account of race, color, or previous condition of servitude."


When you vote, you are an active participant influencing the present and the future by contributing your position on ideas and ideals and supporting candidates that you feel will best represent your interests. If you vote, you can take comfort in the fact that you play a role in the betterment of the political system.

Each state has its own rules and procedures for registering to vote. The rules for New York, New Jersey and Connecticut are set forth below.

Voting in New York
+How do I register to vote?

Registering to Vote in New York.

  • Enter one’s name in the Board of Elections’ mailing list to have a voter registration form mailed to you:http://www.elections.ny.gov/VoterRegFormRequest.html
  • Call New York State Board of Elections at 1-800-FOR-VOTE
  • Register in person at New York State Agency-based voter registration center. For a list of such centers, click here: http://www.elections.ny.gov/NVRA.html
  • Mail a voter registration form to county board of elections. Print one out here and manually complete it: http://www.elections.ny.gov/NYSBOE/download/voting/voteform.pdf
  • Or fill one out online and print here: http://www.elections.ny.gov/NYSBOE/download/voting/voteform_enterable.pdf
+How much time in advance of an election do I register to vote?

In order to vote in most elections, a voter must be registered to vote in the state in which he or she wishes to vote.


In order to vote in the next election in New York, one’s voter registration form should be postmarked 25 days before the election in which one wants to vote and it must be received no less than twenty days before the election.

+How Do I Check to See if I Am Already Registered to Vote?

Use New York State’s Board of Elections link to check: https://voterlookup.elections.state.ny.us/votersearch.aspx

+What If I Am Already Registered and Need to Change My Voting Address?

Fill out a voter registration form as a change of address form; The form must be received at least 20 days before a special, primary, or general election by a county board of elections which must be entered in its records in time for that election (may be easiest to just register at county board of elections if pressed for time). Click here: http://www.elections.ny.gov/CountyBoards.html

+How to Switch One’s Party Affiliation?
  • One can change or affiliate with a party for the first time using a voter registration form;
  • This form must be received no later than 25 days before general election and shall be deposited in a sealed enrollment box and opened for the first time the Tuesday following the general election and entered in voter’s registration record
+What Qualifies as Valid Proof of Eligibility to Vote

Proof of residency can include a government-issued form of ID (e.g., a driver’s license or a passport), and the last four digits of one’s social security number;

Other Requirements:

  • All voters must be U.S. Citizens;
  • One must be 18 years old by December 31 of the year one files one’s voter registration form;
  • One must be 18 years old by the date of the general election, primary or other election in which one wants to vote;
  • One must live at one’s present address at least 30 days before an election in which one wants to vote;
  • One may not be in prison or on parole for a felony conviction;
  • One cannot be found to be mentally incompetent by a court;
  • One cannot claim the right to vote elsewhere
+Can I Vote as an Absentee Voter?

What is an absentee ballot? An absentee ballot is a ballot that allows a voter to vote in a primary and/or general election if he or she is unable to vote where registered. To get an absentee ballot one must be (1) registered to vote and (2) not currently residing or able to vote in one’s “home” voting district.


To qualify to vote absentee, one must be:

  • absent from one’s home county or, if a resident of New York City, absent from the City on Election Day;
  • Unable to appear at the polls due to temporary or permanent illness or disability;
  • A primary care giver of one or more individuals who are ill or physically disabled;
  • A patient or inmate in a Veterans’ Administration Hospital.
  • Detained in jail awaiting Grand Jury action or confined in prison after conviction for an offense other than a felony.
  • For more information see: http://www.elections.ny.gov/VotingAbsentee.html
+How Do You Vote?

First, you have to verify your voter registration and locate your specific polling place. Once that location is found, go to the polls and give your name to the inspector and sign the poll roster book. You will then receive the paper ballot. Next, cast your vote. There are several different types of machines that are available in New York depending on the county you where you are registered to vote. If at any time you need help casting your vote, feel free to ask a poll inspector if you need assistance. You may be assisted by any person of your choice including a trained poll worker, but you are not permitted to obtain assistance from your employer or union representative

+How do I register to vote?
  • Complete a voter’s registration form and mail to your town hall or voter’s registration agency (have postmarked 5th day before primary election and the 7th day before a general election) http://www.sots.ct.gov/sots/lib/sots/electionservices/electforms/electforms/voter_reg_card_english_2015.pdf
  • Complete a voter’s registration form in person at a designated voting location in your town the day of the election

+How Do You Vote?

First, you must make sure that you are properly registered and know the specific location of your polling place. Next, upon arriving at the polling place, register with the voting inspectors providing them with your name and address. They will then prompt you to sign the voting authority pad and use this signature to check for a match with the signature provided with your voting registration. If the signature does not match or you have missing/ incorrect information, you will be asked to fill out a Paper Provisional Ballot when you cannot provide proper identification.

For more information on Paper Provisional Ballots see the Voters Rights Handbook and the Department of State Division of Elections. Otherwise, you may cast your vote by using an electronic voting machine and select your candidates, writing-in candidates, or choose your issue using the screen while casting your vote by selecting the red button on the bottom of the screen. One can view an instructional video on the more common voting machine here. You are only allowed assistance in the voting booth if you are blind, visually impaired, physically disabled, or illiterate. Such assistance can be chosen by yourself if you qualify, or if you come alone, two board members will assist you.
